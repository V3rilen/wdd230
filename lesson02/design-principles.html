Visual Hiarchy
Bunkhouseapts.com Bunkhouse Apartments

In this example they make the most important information the largest, which in this case is the maintenance requests. The next biggest element is the buttons, which the user is likely to use a lot on the page. And finally the smallest elements are the navigation tabs that many user wont use.

White Space and Clean Design
Google Google

In this example Google uses whitespace to draw the users attention to the most important element on the page, the search bar. Followed by other smaller and spaced out elements such as the tab count at the top-right, quick navigation links below the search bar, and news at the very bottom.

Repetition
WAVV.com WAVV

In this final example, the website uses repatition to help the user see their product in many ways. They start out by saying what they have to offer, and providing a CTA to request a demo, then they provide many visuals to show the user what their product looks like and what it can do, and finally they have some reviews to highlight the customer appreciation of the product.
